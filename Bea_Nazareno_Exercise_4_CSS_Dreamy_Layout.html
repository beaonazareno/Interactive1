Hyperlinks aren’t just
the skeleton of the web:
They are its eyes,
a path to its soul.






Some networks like Twitter treat hyperlinks a little... better.
Others, insecure social services, are far more paranoid.
Instagram — which is owned by Facebook — does not allow
its audiences to leave the application whatsoever.


You can put up a web address alongside your photos, but it will not go anywhere.
A lot of people start their daily online routines in these cul - de - sacs
of social media, and their journeys end right there.
Many of them do not even realize that they are using
the Internet’s infrastructure whenever they like an Instagram
photograph or leave a comment on a friend’s Facebook video.


And a blind webpage, one without hyperlinks, can't look
or gaze at another webpage - and this has some serious
consequences for the dynamics of power on the web.
